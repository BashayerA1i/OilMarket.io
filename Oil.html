How Covid-19 affect oil mark ?

Oil prices decline of 8.0% affecting by the pandemic

Usually drops in oil prices is an excellent news for downstream companies since they benefit
greatly from reduced input costs. Indeed, oil prices dropped because lingering oversupply but more importantly because of severe drops in the demand
for derived products from oil,
such as gasoline, diesel and kerosene.

In early March 2020
OPEC officials presented Russia
with an ultimatum to cut production
by 1.5% of world supply.
At the end of March
‘ OPEC+ ’ agreement expire cutting oil production by 2.1 million barrels a day. Saudi Arabia, which made a disproportionate amount of cuts to persuade Russia to stay in the deal announced to its buyers that they would increase production and cut their oil in April. This resulted in a more than 30% Brent crude price drop before a slight recovery and widespread turmoil in the financial markets
